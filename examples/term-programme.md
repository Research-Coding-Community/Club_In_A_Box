# Example Term Programme

This example assumes a weekly anchor day plus optional satellite sessions.

| Week | Format | Topic | Lead role |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Welcome and co-working | What the club is for, member needs, shared notes | Convenor |
| 2 | Clinic | Bring a coding, package, data, or workflow question | Facilitator and helpers |
| 3 | Tutorial | Reproducible project structure | Facilitator |
| 4 | Show-and-tell | Three five-minute demos from members | Notes lead |
| 5 | Co-working | Focused work with helper support | Helper |
| 6 | Discussion | What makes technical work visible and recognised? | Convenor |
| 7 | Tutorial | Version control or collaborative workflows | Facilitator |
| 8 | Clinic | Debugging and dependency problems | Helpers |
| 9 | Cross-club session | Shared problems across related communities | Club leads |
| 10 | Retrospective | Stop, start, continue and next term planning | Evaluation lead |

## Notes

- Keep one session per term explicitly beginner-friendly.
- Include at least one session led by a new facilitator.
- Protect one session for community feedback and planning.
- Publish the programme early, even if some topics are provisional.
