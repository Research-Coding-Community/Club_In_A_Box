# Facilitation Guide

Facilitation is the work of making a session useful for the people in the room. It is not the same as being the most expert person there.

## Before The Session

- Confirm the room, online link, accessibility information, and timing.
- Check that any practical material runs on a clean machine or cloud environment.
- Decide how questions will be handled.
- Prepare a shared notes document.
- Identify at least one helper if the session is practical.
- Decide what can be skipped if time is short.

## At The Start

- Say who the session is for.
- Make the level clear.
- Explain how to ask for help.
- Point to the shared notes.
- Invite people to step out, take breaks, or participate quietly.
- Name the planned finish time and stick to it.

## During Practical Work

- Use short instruction blocks followed by time to try things.
- Pause regularly to check pace.
- Repeat questions before answering.
- Avoid putting individuals on the spot.
- Keep an issue list for problems that cannot be solved immediately.
- Use helpers to support one-to-one questions.

## During Discussion

- Make space for newer voices before returning to frequent speakers.
- Ask people to define acronyms and local terms.
- Capture decisions and actions in the shared notes.
- Separate "ideas to explore" from "actions someone has agreed to do".

## Closing The Session

- Summarise useful links, decisions, and next steps.
- Ask for one piece of feedback.
- Invite future topics.
- Thank helpers and contributors by name if they are comfortable with that.
- Confirm when notes will be shared.

## Useful Phrases

- "Let's define that term before we move on."
- "I am going to pause there and check the pace."
- "That sounds useful, but outside today's scope. I will add it to the follow-up list."
- "Does someone who has not spoken yet want to come in?"
- "It is fine to follow along quietly; participation does not have to mean speaking."
