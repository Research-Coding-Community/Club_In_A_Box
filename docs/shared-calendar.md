# Shared Calendar

A shared calendar helps several clubs behave like a connected community without forcing them into one structure.

## What To Publish

For each event, publish:

- Title.
- Club or group name.
- Date, time, and timezone.
- Location or online link route.
- Audience and experience level.
- Format.
- Registration link if used.
- Contact route.
- Accessibility notes.
- Link to materials after the event.

Use the calendar entry template.

## Calendar Governance

Keep the calendar lightweight:

- One named maintainer checks quality and duplication.
- Each club owns its own event details.
- Events should be added at least two weeks ahead where possible.
- Cancelled or changed events should be marked clearly.
- Past events should keep links to materials where available.

## Cadence

A reliable pattern helps people plan. Examples:

- Weekly anchor day for co-working, demos, or clinics.
- Monthly club-specific sessions.
- Termly showcase or cross-club event.
- Annual retrospective or planning workshop.

## Avoid Calendar Sprawl

If the community has many events, tag them by:

- Topic.
- Skill level.
- Delivery mode.
- Discipline.
- Club.
- Newcomer-friendly status.
