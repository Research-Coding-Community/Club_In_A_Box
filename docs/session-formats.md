# Session Formats

Use this menu when planning a term programme.

## Co-Working

Participants bring their own work and share a room or online space. Helpers are available for questions.

Good for:

- Building routine.
- Reducing isolation.
- Supporting mixed skill levels.

## Clinic

Participants bring questions, bugs, installation problems, workflow issues, or design problems.

Good for:

- Practical problem solving.
- Finding common training needs.
- Making expertise visible.

## Tutorial

A facilitator leads a structured activity.

Good for:

- Introducing a tool or method.
- Creating reusable materials.
- Supporting beginners.

## Show-And-Tell

Several people give short demos of tools, workflows, packages, or lessons learned.

Good for:

- Raising visibility.
- Encouraging first-time contributors.
- Sharing practice across disciplines.

## Reading Or Code Group

The group discusses an article, package, notebook, dataset, or codebase.

Good for:

- Deeper technical discussion.
- Connecting research practice to implementation.
- Supporting advanced members without excluding beginners.

## Co-Design Workshop

Members identify needs, test ideas, and shape the club model.

Good for:

- Building ownership.
- Improving inclusion.
- Packaging lessons for reuse.

See the run a co-design workshop playbook.
