# Evaluation

Evaluation should help the club learn, not create paperwork that prevents activity.

## Useful Questions

Track a small number of questions consistently:

- Who is attending?
- Which disciplines, roles, or groups are not yet represented?
- Are sessions useful?
- Are beginners comfortable joining?
- Are leadership tasks rotating?
- Are people using the materials after sessions?
- Are other clubs reusing or adapting the resources?

## Short-Term Measures

- Number of sessions delivered.
- Attendance and repeat attendance.
- Number of disciplines, teams, or roles represented.
- Survey response rate.
- New topics requested.
- Number of helpers or facilitators involved.

## Medium-Term Measures

- Clubs reusing templates.
- Sessions delivered by new convenors.
- Shared calendar adoption.
- Cross-club collaborations.
- Resources reused outside the original institution.

## Long-Term Measures

- New convenors supported into leadership roles.
- Sustained session cadence after the founding period.
- Adoption by another institution or network.
- Evidence that members feel less isolated.
- Improvements to research practice, reproducibility, or confidence.

## Feedback Rhythm

- One-minute feedback after every session.
- Short survey once per term.
- Stop/start/continue retrospective every six months.
- Annual summary of learning and reusable outputs.

Use the feedback form, survey template, and retrospective template.
