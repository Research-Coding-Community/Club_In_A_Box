# Inclusion And Accessibility

Inclusive clubs are designed so people can join without already knowing the organisers, the jargon, the tools, or the hidden rules.

## Make Participation Clear

Every event page should state:

- Who the session is for.
- Expected experience level.
- Whether people need to prepare.
- What software or accounts are required.
- Whether laptops are needed.
- How to ask questions before the event.
- How to request adjustments.

## Reduce Barriers

- Offer beginner routes and advanced routes where possible.
- Avoid assuming everyone can install software on managed devices.
- Provide slides, notebooks, links, or notes before or after the session.
- Avoid food-only networking if some people cannot attend catering-based events.
- Make online participation meaningful when hybrid is offered.
- Schedule around known teaching, clinical, caring, or commute constraints where possible.

## Facilitate For Different Confidence Levels

- Use plain language.
- Define acronyms.
- Avoid jokes based on people not knowing something.
- Give people time to think before asking for answers.
- Provide anonymous or low-pressure ways to ask questions.
- Treat errors as normal parts of technical work.

## Data And Privacy

- Do not ask people to share sensitive research data in open sessions.
- Use toy datasets or public examples when teaching.
- Aggregate or anonymise survey responses.
- Explain how feedback will be used.
- Do not publish attendee lists without permission.

## Physical And Digital Access

Use the accessibility checklist before each event. At minimum, check:

- Step-free access.
- Room capacity and seating.
- Audio and screen visibility.
- Captions or transcript options for online delivery.
- Breaks for longer sessions.
- Dietary requirements where catering is provided.
- Clear joining instructions.
