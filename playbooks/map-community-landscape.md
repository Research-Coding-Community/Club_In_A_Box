# Playbook: Map The Community Landscape

## Goal

Understand who is doing digital research technical work, which communities already exist, where people feel isolated, and what support is missing.

## What To Map

- Existing clubs, networks, seminars, clinics, and training programmes.
- Convenors and informal leaders.
- Disciplines, departments, institutes, and professional services teams.
- Roles and career stages.
- Common tools, languages, platforms, and workflows.
- Known gaps.
- Groups that are underrepresented in current activity.
- Opportunities for shared calendars, joint sessions, or resource reuse.

## Methods

Use a mix of:

- Short survey.
- Interviews with club leads.
- Open drop-in discussion.
- Desk research of websites and mailing lists.
- Snowball sampling: ask each contact who else should be included.

Use the community map template and survey template.

## Keep It Ethical

- Explain why information is being collected.
- Avoid publishing identifiable feedback without consent.
- Store personal contact details securely.
- Report patterns in aggregate.
- Be clear that the map will be incomplete and open to correction.

## Outputs

The map should produce:

- A list of existing groups and contacts.
- A list of unmet needs.
- Candidate topics for sessions.
- Candidate helpers or facilitators.
- A view of duplicated effort.
- Recommendations for the first shared calendar or anchor day.

## Review Questions

- Which communities are already strong?
- Which groups are missing?
- Where is there demand but no current provision?
- Where can existing clubs share materials?
- What would make the biggest difference in the next three months?
